Experience


  • 2007-2010

    Cashier at A&W

    Like most people, I started out with an entry level job in the fast food service. Back then I was super shy, awkward, and not that great at talking with people. Over the 3+ years, I learned the art of small talk and gained some pretty decent communication skills.


  • September 2011

    Co-op at the Center for High-Throughput Biology (CHiBi)

    During my co-ops at CHiBi, I was a bioinformatics research assistant (8 months) and systems administrator assistant (4 months). I learned what bioinformatics is and how to program using a computer. The best piece of advice I was given, was that "if you find yourself doing the same thing more than 3 times, there is probably a programmatic way of accomplishing the same task."


  • September 2013

    Research assistant at the BC Cancer Agency (BCCA)

    After graduating with a B.Sc., my first non-undergraduate level job was at the BCCA running pipelines, parsing data, and generating results. I met many staff, students, and senior research scientists. I wanted to learn more and so applied to graduate school.


  • September 2014 - Present

    Graduate school

    Classes, homework, exams. Life as a student is much as I remembered it to be. Despite the similarities, class sizes were smaller, research projects were more collaborative, and the sense of belonging is much greater. I attended a couple conferences, presented a couple posters, and acquired a couple scholarships. But now the journey is nearing its final chapters and I am excited for what the future holds.
